Research Questions

  1. What is the relationship between the amount of sunlight a plant prefers and the temperature class one may prefer?

  2. What characteristics of edible plants covary together?

Data Munging

library(tidyverse)

subset <- edible_plants %>%
  select(where(is.character))

bivariate <- subset %>% select(sunlight, temperature_class) %>% drop_na()

multivariate <- subset %>% select(cultivation, sunlight, water, nutrients, soil, season, temperature_class) %>% drop_na()

Bar Plot

bivariate %>% ggplot() + geom_bar(aes(x=temperature_class, fill=sunlight), position="dodge") + theme_bw() + labs(x="Temperature Class", y="Count", fill="Sunlight") + theme(legend.position = "top")

Fisher’s Test

Here, I choose to use a Fisher’s Test because the data are sparse in certain categories. In other words, there are less than 5 counts across greater than 20% of the possible combinations of values.

There is a statistically significant association between sunlight required and temperature hardiness.

janitor::fisher.test(bivariate$sunlight, bivariate$temperature_class, simulate.p.value=T)

Interpretations

  1. In the bivariate analysis, there does appear to be a statistically significant relationship between sunlight requirements and temperature hardiness. Caution should be taken because of the small sample sizes across the categories.

  2. The MCA analyses corroborate the above analyses and show that there is a relationship between water and nutrient demands, the soil types and season of planting, and it appears most cultivation(s) follow these patterns. That said, sunlight and temperature appear slightly offset indicating variability across categories. There is no clear pattern by cultivation type as all categories / ellipses overlap in the individual plot with ellipses.
